Swami Vivekananda

The man who was an indian monk, philosopher, author and religious teacher




"Great men are those who build highways for others with their heart's blood." -Swami Vivekananda

Here is a time line of Swami Vivekananda's life:

  • 1863 - SWami Vivekananda was born in calcutta, india as Narendranath Dutta.
  • 1869 - At the age of 6, Swami Vivekananda goes to pathshala, a school where kids learn sanskrit.
  • 1871 - Swami Vivekananda enters the Metropolitan Institute.
  • 1879 - Swami Vivekananda goes to the Presidency College.
  • 1881 - Swami Vivekananda transfers to the General Assembly Institution, meets Sri Ramakrishna.
  • 1893 - Swami Vivekananda arrives in Chicago to represent Hinduism and deliver his message to the other cultures.
  • 1897 - Swami Vivekananda creates the Ramakrishna Mission, an organization that focuses on different social services.
  • 1899 - Swami Vivekananda creates Belur Math, an organization that focused on monastic life of monks.
  • 1902 - At the age of just 39, Swami Vivekananda dies.
